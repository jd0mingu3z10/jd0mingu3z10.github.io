The YouTube video shown Web 2.0 the machine is Us/ing Us is a great video and the part where he visited Yahoo page in the 90s brought back memories. I remember using the internet a lot in elementary school for researching topics for assignments. I remember the layout and how the hypertext links were very commonly used. It's cool to see that even though we are in the beginning of making a webpage, that these are the type of codes that were use in order to link to other Pages or other information, kind of a basic layout, and I definitely have much a better idea how it was put together now. One term that caught my attention because I've seen it plenty throughout my time is XML.

 

So why was XML important? XML stands for extensible markup language. The difference between XML and HTML is that HTML is used for displaying information while XML is used to carry information. The purpose for XML is that it's used for structured data to be stored and transported.

Another term that I wanted to look into is "web mashups". According to whatis.techtarget.com, mashup is a web page or application that integrates complimentary elements from two or more sources.

Some of the best web mashups years ago before smartphone apps were weatherbonk, housing Maps, find nearby, mapdango, and others more. WeatherBonk was used to see weather and traffic conditions, commonly used by a weather forecaster or traffic reporter. Combining Google Maps data with weather resources including WeatherBug and National Weather Service, weatherbonk was the best mashup for a weather forecaster. Which also offered traffic details. Housing Maps very much like Zillow was a good mashup for people looking to buy a new home. Housing Maps gathering information from Craigslist and combined it with Google Maps. Making it easier for someone to look for a house to rent or for sale. So you can kind of see where 2 sources were used to help with these websites.
